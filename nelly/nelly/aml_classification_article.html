Anti Money-Laundering detector



A Machine Learning Case Study for AML at Betclic based on financial teams feedback.

Business problem¶

Betclic, as a major internationnal gambling plateform, is constantly targeted by fraud, especially money-laundering.
To counter it, Betclic in agreement with the Supervisory Authority in Malta, implement solution to detect early suspicious act of malicious financial behavior.
Our aim here is to use data analysis and machine learning tools to detect high risk customers in order to report it to the financial department.

Problem statement¶

As regulation request it, customers have been attributed a rating from 1 to 5 in order to mesure their level of risk.
1 being the lowest level and 5 means that the account must be close.
Actually, Betclic can only rate customer from 1 to 4, 5 being set by the decision of an exterior verification actor.
For now, each rated customer has been checked by hand by the financial department.

Objective and constraintes¶

Objective¶

  • Detection of high risk customers (risk rating 4 and 5)

Constraintes¶

  • low volume of data
  • bias in the data itself : all rated customer has been processed because of abnormal financial behavior (ARGEL request, internal detection, etc)

In [48]:
dict_score = {}

for treshold in np.arange(0, 1, 0.05):
    treshold_round = np.round(treshold, 2)
    tmp = sc.score(y_tot_test, np.asarray([1 if x[1]> treshold_round else 0 for x in forest_tot.predict_proba(X_tot_test)]))
    dict_score[treshold_round] = {}
    dict_score[treshold_round]['recall'] = tmp['recall_score']
    dict_score[treshold_round]['precision'] = tmp['precision_score']

Other experiments : Clustering¶

To increase our volume of data, we apply a clustering and check if we have clusters with a rich concentration of high risk customers. Infortunatly, this methode gives us two possibles results:

  • we detect a tiny part of our hight risk customer and, among those detected, we are 50% confident about our prediction.
  • we detect the large majority of our high risk clients, but we are 40% confident about our prediction and almost 78% of all customers are considered as suspicious.
